Introduction


Undeniably one of the greatest challenges of the 21st century, Climate Change is a matter of global concern. As a result, numerous countries around the world have begun to make preventing climate change their number 1 priority.

Unfortunately, Australia is not one of such nations. Having stalled and resisted commitment to targets consistently, Australia’s climate change policy leaves much to be desired. However, if climate change is so serious why does Australia keep being allowed to stall, and exactly how bad is it?

In this visualisation I aim to explore these questions by considering some of the common excuses used to delay climate action, providing a look into what must be done, and how that may be achieved.
